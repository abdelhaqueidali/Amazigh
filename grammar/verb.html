Conjugation
ⴰⵙⴼⵜⵉ

- Imperative forms do not have their own verb forms. They use the available forms as follows:
• Imperative ⴰⵏⴰⴹ uses the Aorist form ⵓⵔⵎⵉⵔ
• Emphatic Imperative ⴰⵏⴰⴹ ⵓⵙⵙⵉⴷ uses the Imperfective form ⴰⵔⵓⵙⵎⵉⴷ.
- The verb in imperative conjugations for the singular masculine and singular feminine pronouns does not carry any verbal affixes.
- The imperative form is not available for all pronouns, it is only available for you (singular masculine), you (singular feminine), you (plural masculine), and you (plural feminine).

Changes
ⴰⵙⵏⴼⵍ


- If the verb has one of the vowels ⴰ, ⵉ, ⵓ at its beginning, then the verbal affix for the pronoun ⵏⵜⵜⴰ changes from ⵉ to ⵢ.

ⵓⵎⵥ ("ⴰⵎⵥ" ⴳ ⵓⵙⵎⵉⴷ) => ⵏⵜⵜⴰ ⵉⵓⵎⵥ => ⵏⵜⵜⴰ ⵢⵓⵎⵥ.
ⴰⵡⵉ => ⴰⴷ ⵉⴰⵡⵉ ⵏⵜⵜⴰ => ⴰⴷ ⵢⴰⵡⵉ.



- If the verb has ⵜⵜ at its beginning, then the verbal affixes for the pronouns ⴽⵢⵢ, ⴽⵎⵎ, ⴽⵏⵏⵉ, ⴽⵏⵏⵉⵏⵜⵉ change from ⵜ to ⵜⴻ.

• ⵜⵜⵓ => ⴽⵢⵢ ⵜⵜⵜⵓⴷ => ⴽⵢⵢ ⵜⴻⵜⵜⵓⴷ.
• ⵜⵜⵉⵏⵉ => ⴰⴷ ⵜⵜⵜⵉⵏⵉⴷ => ⴰⴷ ⵜⴻⵜⵜⵉⵏⵉⴷ.


- If the verb has ⵜⵜⴻⵜⵜ at its beginning, then the previous verbal affixes ⵜ for the pronouns ⴽⵢⵢ, ⴽⵎⵎ, ⴽⵏⵏⵉ, ⴽⵏⵏⵉⵏⵜⵉ are not added. This issue concerns ⵜⵜⵓ in the imperfective form ⵜⵜⴻⵜⵜⵓ.

ⵜⵜⴻⵜⵜⵓ ("ⵜⵜⵓ" ⴳ ⴰⵔⵓⵙⵎⵉⴷ) => ⴽⵢⵢ ⵜⵜⴻⵜⵜⵓⴷ.

- If the verb starts with ⵏ‌ⵏ, we add ⵏⴻ instead of ⵏ only in the conjugation of the verb for the pronoun ⵏⴽⴽⵏⵉ.

- ⵏⵏⵉ\ⵏⵏⴰ ("ⵉⵏⵉ" ⴳ ⵓⵙⵎⵉⴷ) => ⵏⴽⴽⵏⵉ ⵏ‌ⵏⵏⴰ => ⵏⴽⴽⵏⵉ ⵏⴻⵏⵏⴰ

Tenses

Past
- The perfective and negative perfective forms are used to talk about an action that happened in the past. As the name suggests, the perfective indicates completion and finishing.
Examples:
ⵖⵔⵉⵖ ⴰⴷⵍⵉⵙ ⴰⴷ ⵉⴹⴳⴰⵎ.
ⵓⵔ ⵖⵔⵉⵖ ⴰⴷⵍⵉⵙ ⴰⴷ.
All other forms are negated by adding the particle ⵓⵔ with the same forms, except for the perfective. There is a negative perfective to use with the negation particle ⵓⵔ.

Present
- The aorist and imperfective forms are used with the present.
Examples:
ⵔⵉⵖ ⴰⴷ ⵖⵔⵖ ⴰⴷⵍⵉⵙ ⴰⴷ.
ⴷⴰ ⵇⵇⴰⵔⵖ ⴽⵔⴰ ⵙⴳ ⵓⴷⵍⵉⵙ ⴰⴷ ⴽⵔⴰⵢⴳⴰⵜ ⴰⵙⵙ.

Future
- The aorist and imperfective forms are used with the future.
ⵔⴰⴷ ⵖⵔⵖ ⴰⴷⵍⵉⵙ ⴰⴷ.
ⵔⴰⴷ ⵇⵇⴰⵔⵖ ⵉⵎⵉⴽⴽ ⵙⴳ ⵓⴷⵍⵉⵙ ⴰⴷ ⴽⵔⴰⵢⴳⴰⵜ ⴰⵙⵙ.

Imperative
- Imperative forms are used to give a command. There is a regular imperative and an emphatic imperative.
ⵖⵔ ⴰⴷⵍⵉⵙ ⴰⴷ!
ⵇⵇⴰⵔ ⴰⴷⵍⵉⵙ ⴰⴷ!

Contexts of the Subjunctive Form
- This form appears in different places, such as the use of the particles ⵍⵍⵉ, ⴷⴰ, ⵏⵏⴰ.

ⵓⵙⵎⵉⴷ Perfective: ⴰⴼⵔⵓⵅ ⵍⵍⵉ ⵉⵖⵔⴰⵏ
ⵓⵙⵎⵉⴷ ⴰⵏⴰⴱⴰⵡ Negative Perfective: ⴰⴼⵔⵓⵅ ⴷⴰ ⵓⵔ ⵉⵖⵔⵉⵏ
ⴰⵔⵓⵙⵎⵉⴷ Imperfective: ⴰⴼⵔⵓⵅ ⵏⵏⴰ ⵢⴰⵇⵇⵔⴰⵏ

Attention!

- When one of the verbal affixes is the same as the letter the verb begins with, a person often forgets to add the verbal affix without realizing that the letter in the verb is just a root letter and not a verbal affix:

Examples

- ⵉⵙⵙⴰⵏ => ⴰⴷ ⵉⵙⵙⴰⵏ‌ⵏ (ⵏⵉⵜⵏⵉ) ✅
- ⴰⴷ ⵉⵙⵙⴰⵏ (ⵏⵉⵜⵏⵉ) ❌

- ⵉⵍⵉ => ⵔⴰⴷ ⵢⵉⵍⵉ (ⵏⵜⵜⴰ). ✅
- ⵉⵍⵉ => ⴰⴷ ⵉⵍⵉ ⵏⵜⵜⴰ ❌

- ⴼⴼⵖ => ⵏⴽⴽ ⵔⴰⴷ ⴼⴼⵖⵖ ✅
- ⵏⴽⴽ ⵔⴰⴷ ⴼⴼⵖ ❌
